## Design Context

### Users
This portfolio is primarily for developers interested in the project as an AI-powered portfolio demo, potential hiring managers evaluating Lindsay Spencer, and people who appreciate strong web application design. They are typically using it to quickly understand Lindsay's technical capability, design taste, and the quality of the interactive experience.

### Brand Personality
The interface should feel smooth, professional, and creative. It should present Lindsay as a senior frontend engineer who is concise, technically credible, and intentional about craft. The emotional goal is confidence first, with creativity expressed through polished interaction and presentation rather than visual noise.

### Aesthetic Direction
Favor light mode as the primary experience. The portfolio should feel sleek and modern, with refined motion, strong visual hierarchy, and deliberate product-style execution. It should showcase AI-assisted interaction and frontend design skill without drifting into generic AI aesthetics or novelty for its own sake.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with competence: help visitors understand Lindsay's skills, judgment, and project impact quickly.
2. Keep it smooth, not flashy: motion and interaction should feel polished and intentional, never distracting.
3. Use creativity with restraint: the design should feel distinctive and memorable while staying professional.
4. Prioritize clarity in hiring contexts: content structure, labels, and navigation should support rapid evaluation.
5. Treat the portfolio itself as proof of craft: every view should reinforce that this is both a case study and a product-quality frontend experience.
